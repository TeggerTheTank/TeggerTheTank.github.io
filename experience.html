Previous Employment

Thomas Egger was hired on as a student worker during his second semester at McHenry County College. During this time he operated technical equipment ranging from Mac computers to video recording equipment. His tasks were met with enthusiasm and precision. His duties had him regularly tutoring students in the proper use of many software programs, including Adobe Design Suite, Microsoft Office, and Unreal Engine. He also tutored students in the proper application of HTML, CSS, Javascript and C++.

Thomas Egger was voted into office by his pears as the leader of the MCC Game Club. His goal was to turn an unorganized group of individules into a productive, and well-adjusted team. He accomplished this by setting a single goal for the group, and leading them toward it. Through the efforts of this new team they created, published, and sold a card game, Animal Hackers, which has seen critical success.

Thomas began work at brown printing in 2012 as a Jogger. A Joggers responsibilities were wide-reaching and varied. From cleaning to maintaining the press, he met all tasks with efficiency and enthusiasm. His main responsibility was the maintenance and operation of a robotic arm that would stack print products onto pallets.
